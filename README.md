## How to use this list

Do not read it top to bottom. Use it like a menu.

1. Find your role in [Prep by role](#prep-by-role). That is your anchor.
2. Pull the matching company guide from [Prep by company](#prep-by-company). Know the exact loop before you study anything.
3. Drill your weakest skill from [Prep by skill](#prep-by-skill).
4. Practice out loud with [mock interviews](#practice-and-mock-interviews), not just in your head.
5. Pick a timeline from [Study plans](#study-plans) and stick to it.

The candidates who land offers are not the ones who study the most. They are the ones who study the right thing for the loop in front of them.

## Prep by skill

### SQL

The skill that fails more strong candidates than any other. Get fast and clean under pressure.

- [Dataford question bank](https://dataford.io/questions) - real SQL questions from top companies, run against live datasets with instant AI feedback.
- [Mode SQL Tutorial](https://mode.com/sql-tutorial/) - free, practical, analyst-focused.
- [SQLBolt](https://sqlbolt.com/) - interactive lessons that start from zero.
- [PostgreSQL Mastery Cheat Sheet](https://dataford.io/blog) - SELECT to window functions in one reference.

### Python and coding

For data roles the bar is clean, correct, readable code. For software roles it goes deeper into algorithms.

- [NeetCode](https://neetcode.io/) - the coding patterns roadmap, free and well structured.
- [LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/) - the standard practice bank. Filter by company and difficulty.
- [HackerRank](https://www.hackerrank.com/) - good for timed drills and SQL crossover.

### Statistics and probability

Tested heavily for data science and quant roles. Focus on intuition, not memorized formulas.

- [StatQuest with Josh Starmer](https://www.youtube.com/c/joshstarmer) - the clearest stats and ML explanations on the internet.
- [Khan Academy: Statistics and Probability](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/statistics-probability) - solid free foundation.
- [Seeing Theory](https://seeing-theory.brown.edu/) - visual, intuitive probability.

### Machine learning

Know the fundamentals cold. Be ready to explain trade-offs, not just name algorithms.

- [Designing Machine Learning Systems](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/designing-machine-learning/9781098107956/) by Chip Huyen - the modern ML systems reference.
- [Chip Huyen's blog](https://huyenchip.com/blog/) - sharp writing on ML in production.
- [StatQuest ML playlist](https://www.youtube.com/c/joshstarmer) - decision trees, boosting, and more, explained simply.

### A/B testing and experimentation

The differentiator for product data science. Most candidates underprepare here.

- [Trustworthy Online Controlled Experiments](https://experimentguide.com/) by Kohavi, Tang, and Xu - the definitive book on A/B testing.
- [Dataford mock interviews](https://dataford.io/) - practice experimentation cases graded by AI.

### Product and business sense

As AI absorbs more execution work, this is where offers are won. Learn to turn vague problems into clear data questions.

- [Dataford mock interviews](https://dataford.io/) - timed product and business cases with per-question feedback.
- [Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/) - product thinking from operators.
- [Stellar Peers](https://stellarpeers.com/) - product case frameworks and examples.

### System design

Required for software, ML, and data engineering loops at scale.

- [System Design Primer](https://github.com/donnemartin/system-design-primer) - the classic open-source starting point.
- [Designing Data-Intensive Applications](https://dataintensive.net/) by Martin Kleppmann - essential for data engineering and ML systems.

### Behavioral

Underrated and very coachable. Use a tight structure and real stories.

- [STAR method overview](https://www.themuse.com/advice/star-interview-method) - the standard framework for structuring answers.
- [Amazon Leadership Principles](https://www.amazon.jobs/content/en/our-workplace/leadership-principles) - the template many companies quietly borrow from.
- [Dataford behavioral question bank](https://dataford.io/questions) - real behavioral prompts with model frameworks.

## Study plans

Pick the runway that matches your timeline. Then protect the time.

**4 weeks (you have an interview scheduled)**

- Week 1: Pull your exact [company and role guide](https://dataford.io/interview-guides). Map the loop. Drill SQL daily.
- Week 2: Your weakest core skill (stats, ML, or system design). Two hours a day.
- Week 3: Product and business cases out loud. Record yourself. Get feedback.
- Week 4: Full [mock interviews](https://dataford.io/), behavioral stories, and rest before the loop.

**8 weeks (you are getting ready to apply)**

- Weeks 1 to 2: Foundations. SQL and Python fluency until they feel automatic.
- Weeks 3 to 4: Statistics, experimentation, and ML fundamentals.
- Weeks 5 to 6: Product sense and case practice. Build a story bank of 8 to 10 examples.
- Weeks 7 to 8: Company-specific prep and repeated mock interviews under time pressure.

The goal is not to finish the list. It is to be ready for the loop in front of you.
